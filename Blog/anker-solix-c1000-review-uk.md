## Battery Chemistry: Why LFP Matters

The SOLIX C1000 Gen 2 uses **Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP)**, not the nickel-cobalt chemistry (NMC) found in older Jackery models. This matters because:

**LFP advantages:**
- **Longer lifespan** — 3,000–5,000 cycles vs 1,000–2,000 for NMC
- **Better cold weather performance** — maintains 80%+ capacity in 0°C conditions
- **Safer chemistry** — lower thermal runaway risk, more stable under load
- **Better cycle degradation** — loses only 10–15% capacity over 3,000 cycles, vs 25–30% for NMC

**For plug-in solar:** If you're using the battery daily (charging during the day, discharging at night), LFP chemistry means your battery will outlast your solar panels. You're looking at 8–10 years of reliable service with the SOLIX C1000 Gen 2.

## Real-World Performance with Plug-in Solar

Here's how the Anker SOLIX C1000 Gen 2 performs paired with an 800W plug-in solar system:

**On a sunny day (summer):**
- 9am–5pm: Your 800W solar system feeds the battery
- You charge the battery from empty to full in approximately 1.3 hours (1,056Wh ÷ 800W)
- Evening (5pm–midnight): You run appliances from battery discharge (1,200W continuous available)

**On an average day (spring/autumn):**
- Solar generation: ~400W average
- Full charge time: approximately 2.6 hours
- Evening draw: 1–1.5kWh per household

**On a grey day (winter):**
- Solar generation: ~100W average
- Battery acts as daily buffer, storing morning/noon generation for afternoon/evening use
- Critical for offsetting peak tariffs (Economy 7, Agile, etc.)

## The Case for Pairing with Plug-in Solar

Plug-in solar + battery creates a powerful combination because:

1. **Time-shifting:** Solar generates midday. You use power 6pm–11pm. A battery bridges this gap.
2. **Off-peak arbitrage:** On Agile tariffs (Octopus Energy), you can store cheap midday solar and avoid expensive peak rates.
3. **Resilience:** You have 1kWh of backup power during grid outages or low-solar days.
4. **Higher ROI:** A £800 battery paired with a £1,500 system increases savings from £150/year to £300–£400/year.

With the SOLIX C1000 Gen 2, payback shifts from 10 years (solar alone) to 4–5 years (solar + battery).

## Temperature Performance

The SOLIX C1000 Gen 2's LFP chemistry shines in UK conditions:

| Temperature | Capacity Retention |
|---|---|
| 25°C (room temp) | 100% |
| 15°C (cool room) | 98% |
| 0°C (winter) | 85–90% |
| -10°C (freezing) | 60–70% (recovery after warming) |

In typical British winter conditions (5–10°C), you'll lose only 5–10% capacity. In summer, no loss at all.

**Compare to NMC batteries (Jackery):** They drop to 60–70% capacity at 0°C, making them unsuitable for unheated UK garages.
