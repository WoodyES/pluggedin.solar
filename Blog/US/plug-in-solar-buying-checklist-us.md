## Before You Click Buy: Check These 10 Things

Buying a plug-in solar system is a $1,500–2,000 decision. Don't rush it. Run through this checklist first.

## 1. Is It UL-Listed or UL 3700 Certified?

UL listing means the inverter (and ideally the full system) has passed independent safety testing.

UL 3700 is the new standard for grid-interactive plug-in solar. As of April 2026, no products are fully UL 3700 certified yet, but that's changing by late 2026.

**What to do now:**
- Ask the seller: "Is this UL 3700 certified or expected to be?"
- If not certified, ask for timeline (most say "late 2026").
- If you're buying *now* before certification, get confirmation from a tax pro that federal ITC eligibility won't be affected.

**Red flag:** If a seller doesn't mention UL status or can't explain it, that's suspicious. Pass.

## 2. Anti-Islanding: Does It Shut Down During Grid Outages?

Anti-islanding is a safety feature. If the grid fails, your system automatically stops feeding power into the lines. This protects utility workers.

All reputable systems have anti-islanding. It's non-negotiable.

**What to do:**
- Check the spec sheet or manual
- Look for "grid-interactive" or "grid-tie" language
- Call the company if unclear

**Red flag:** Any system claiming to power your house during a grid outage is likely *not* anti-islanding certified and is a safety liability.

## 3. Monitoring: Is There an App? How Good?

You'll want to track what your system generates.

**Check:**
- Is there an app? (iOS, Android, or both?)
- Does it update in real-time, or once a day?
- Does it show historical data?
- Does it have alerts if the system stops producing?
- Is there a web dashboard if you don't want to use a phone app?

**You want:** Real-time updates, historical tracking, and alerts. Nice-to-have: forecasts and consumption tracking.

**Red flag:** No monitoring capability, or an app that requires creating an account with unclear privacy terms.

## 4. Warranty: How Long, and What's Covered?

**Check:**
- **Panel warranty:** 25–30 years is standard (performance degradation, not full replacement).
- **Inverter warranty:** 5–10 years typical. This is the most likely part to fail.
- **Labor warranty:** Who installs? If you DIY, is labor covered if something breaks?

**Red flag:** Warranty shorter than 5 years. That's weak. 10+ years is excellent.

**Real talk:** Warranty is only valuable if the company is still around to honor it. Craftstrom (5-year history) is safer than a brand new startup.

## 5. Panel Efficiency: Is It Competitive?

Modern residential panels are 21–23% efficient. Anything under 20% is dated; anything over 23% is premium.

Bifacial panels (like Craftstrom) add 8–12% output in the right conditions, which can matter.

**What to do:**
- Check spec sheet for "efficiency" percentage
- Compare bifacial vs. monofacial benefits in your mounting scenario
- Don't obsess—the difference between 21% and 22% efficiency is marginal

**Red flag:** Panels less than 19% efficient. That's old technology.

## 6. Inverter Brand: Is It Well-Known?

Inverter manufacturers matter. Some are battle-tested; others are new and risky.

**Reputable brands:**
- APsystems (used by Craftstrom) — solid track record
- Enphase (premium, very reliable)
- SMA (German, excellent)
- Growatt (popular in Asia, expanding in US, reliable)

**Less-known brands:**
- If the inverter is a no-name brand, be cautious. It might work, but support and replacement parts are risky.

**What to do:**
- Check the spec sheet for inverter brand
- Google the brand + "reviews" to see if there are common complaints
- Avoid "private label" inverters from small companies

**Red flag:** Inverter brand you've never heard of with minimal online reviews. That's risky.

## 7. Cable Quality: Is It Outdoor-Rated?

Your system's cables are exposed to sun, rain, temperature swings.

**Check:**
- Are cables UV-rated?
- Are they rated for outdoor use?
- What gauge (thickness) are they? Thicker is better.
- What's the temperature range they're rated for?

**Red flag:** Indoor-rated cables used outdoors. They'll degrade within 2–3 years. Bad move.

Most name-brand systems use proper outdoor cables. But budget DIY combos from Amazon sometimes skimp. Check.

## 8. GFCI Compatibility: Will It Play Nice With Your Outlet?

GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter) outlets detect electrical imbalances and shut off power. They're a safety feature.

Your plug-in solar system needs to work with GFCI outlets (or you need one installed).

**Check:**
- Does the inverter spec sheet mention GFCI compatibility?
- Are there known issues with the inverter tripping GFCI breakers?

**What to do:**
- If your outlet isn't GFCI-protected, budget $15–30 for a GFCI outlet or $100–200 for an electrician to install one

**Red flag:** Inverter that's known to trip GFCI outlets repeatedly. That's a headache.

## 9. Price per Watt: Is It Competitive?

Current market rates for plug-in solar:
- **Budget (Craftstrom):** $1.50–1.70/watt
- **Mid-range (Bright Saver):** $1.75–2.00/watt
- **Premium (EcoFlow):** $1.58/watt panels + batteries

Anything significantly above $2.00/watt is overpriced unless you're buying aesthetics (Bright Saver design) or battery integration (EcoFlow).

Anything below $1.00/watt might be lower-quality DIY combos.

**What to do:**
- Calculate cost ÷ system wattage
- Compare against the three major brands
- If it's higher, why? (Better warranty? Better support? Better design?)

**Red flag:** $2.50+/watt without obvious premium features. Overpriced.

## 10. Customer Support: Can You Actually Reach Someone?

After you buy, something will eventually need clarification or troubleshooting.

**Check:**
- Is there email support? Phone?
- What's the typical response time?
- Are there user forums or a community?
- Does the company have technicians you can talk to, or is it all FAQ?

**Red flag:** No support channel besides a generic contact form. That's risky.

**Quality support:**
- Craftstrom: Community forums (24–72 hour response)
- Bright Saver: Email and phone (24–48 hours)
- EcoFlow: Professional support, phone and email (same-day typical)

## The Real Checklist (TL;DR)

Before buying, confirm:

1. ✓ UL-listed (ideally UL 3700 path to certification)
2. ✓ Anti-islanding certified
3. ✓ App or monitoring capability
4. ✓ 5+ year warranty (10+ ideal)
5. ✓ Panel efficiency 21%+
6. ✓ Inverter from known brand (APsystems, Enphase, Growatt)
7. ✓ Outdoor-rated cables, UV-resistant
8. ✓ GFCI-compatible
9. ✓ Competitive pricing (~$1.60/watt)
10. ✓ Reachable customer support

If a product fails three or more of these, keep looking.

If it passes all ten, you're good to buy.
