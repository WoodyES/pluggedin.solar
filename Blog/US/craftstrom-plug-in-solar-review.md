## Craftstrom at a Glance

Craftstrom is the most available plug-in solar system in the US as of 2026. They've shipped roughly four thousand systems and have been operating for five years—longer than any competitor.

**Price:** $1.60 per watt
**Availability:** 35 US states
**System size:** 200W, 400W, 800W, 1,200W, 1,600W
**Warranty:** 5 years parts
**UL 3700 status:** Pending (expected late 2026)

The core appeal is simple: it's cheap, modular, and proven. But is it the best plug-in solar system, or just the cheapest?

## What You're Buying

A Craftstrom kit includes:

- **Bifacial solar panels (200W each)** — Capture light from both sides. The "front" side captures direct sun; the "back" captures light reflected off the ground or nearby surfaces.
- **Micro-inverter** — Converts DC power from panels to AC power your outlets use.
- **Mounting hardware** — Adjustable brackets for roofs, balconies, patios, or ground mounting.
- **Cables and connectors** — Outdoor-rated, UV-resistant.
- **Monitoring cable and app** — Track generation in real-time via smartphone.

You assemble it yourself. No electrician required. Installation typically takes 2–4 hours for beginners.

## The Bifacial Advantage

Bifacial panels are Craftstrom's main differentiator. They generate power from both sides.

In ideal scenarios (bright parking lot, white-painted patio), bifacial panels can outperform traditional monofacial panels by 15–20%. The reflected light from the ground boosts output.

In typical residential scenarios (grassy backyard, patio), the gain is smaller: 8–12%.

In worst-case scenarios (installed flat against a roof with no clearance), the back side captures nothing, and you get just the front. No loss, but no bifacial benefit either.

**Real-world impact:** If you have control over mounting location and background (light-colored surface, some clearance from the ground), bifacial makes sense. If you're mounting flush against a roof or dark surface, bifacial's advantage evaporates.

For most balconies and patios with some reflective surface nearby, bifacial adds about 10% to output—meaningful, but not transformative.

## Modularity: The True Strength

Craftstrom's modular design is excellent.

Buy one 200W panel now ($320). See how it works, test it on your outlet, monitor for a month.

Want more power? Add another 200W panel ($320). They wire in parallel; your inverter handles both. Total system: 400W. No need to replace anything.

This matters because:

1. **Lower risk** — You're testing the system before committing to a bigger install.
2. **Staged investment** — Spread costs over time instead of dropping $1,500 upfront.
3. **Flexibility** — If your space limitations change, adjust your system size.

Competitors (Bright Saver, EcoFlow) offer fixed kits. You pick a size; you're committed. Craftstrom lets you iterate.

For renters, apartment dwellers, or anyone uncertain, modularity is huge.

## Real-World Performance

Craftstrom panels are rated 200W, but real-world output varies.

On a clear summer day with good sun angle and clean panels, they'll hit 180–200W at peak (roughly 90–100% of rating).

On a cloudy day, 40–60W.

In winter, 80–120W (lower sun angle, shorter days).

Over a full year, a 200W Craftstrom panel in average US sun generates about 240–280 kWh. That's in line with other 200W panels—nothing special, nothing weak.

Where bifacial helps, you might see 280–320 kWh. Not a massive difference, but real.

One thing to note: panel output degrades over time. Craftstrom rates degradation at 0.5% per year (industry standard). So a panel at year 10 operates at 95% of original capacity. By year 25, about 88%.

This is normal and not a weakness.

## Inverter Quality

Craftstrom uses APsystems micro-inverters—a solid, proven brand. APsystems are used by thousands of installers and have reliable track records.

APsystems inverters are typically 96–97% efficient. That's industry-standard. When you generate 1,000W, roughly 960–970W reaches your outlet. The rest is lost as heat.

These inverters have a 10-year lifespan before needing replacement. Replacement cost: ~$400. Over a system's 25-year life, you'll likely replace the inverter once.

One caveat: Craftstrom's warranty covers the inverter for only 5 years. If it fails in year 7, you're buying a replacement. Other brands (EcoFlow) offer longer warranties.

## Monitoring and App

Craftstrom's monitoring app is functional but basic.

You get:
- Real-time power generation (kW)
- Daily/monthly generation totals
- Historical data
- Alerts if system stops generating (useful for troubleshooting)

What you don't get:
- Forecast data (predicted generation based on weather)
- Consumption tracking (how much you're actually saving)
- Advanced troubleshooting
- App integration with home automation systems

For most people, the basics are enough. You'll check the app a few times a week out of curiosity, then stop. Knowing you're generating 2.5 kW right now is interesting but not critical.

If you want deep energy data, you'd need to add a separate home energy monitor.

## Customer Support: The Weak Link

Craftstrom's support is community-based. You post questions to their forum; other users or Craftstrom staff answer.

Response times: 24–72 hours, typically.

If something fails, you initiate a warranty claim, they mail you a replacement part. You install it. No technician visit.

This works fine for straightforward issues. But if something complex goes wrong, you're on your own.

Compare to Bright Saver or EcoFlow, which offer email and phone support with professional engineers.

For DIY-comfortable people, Craftstrom's support is fine. For people wanting white-glove service, it's a weakness.

## Availability: The Big Picture

Craftstrom ships to 35 states. That's more than competitors, but not nationwide.

Restrictions vary by state regulation. Some states have rules about plug-in solar that require products to meet certain standards before they're legal to sell and install.

UL 3700 certification should remove these barriers. Once certified (late 2026), expect Craftstrom to ship nationwide.

If your state isn't currently supported, Craftstrom's website will tell you—don't try ordering and hope.

## Installation: Is It Really DIY?

Yes, mostly. Here's what you need to do:

**Physical assembly (1–2 hours):**
- Bolt panels to mounting brackets
- Run cables from panel to inverter
- Connect inverter to outlet

**Electrical (30 minutes):**
- Ensure your outlet is GFCI-protected
- Plug in the inverter
- Power on

**App setup (15 minutes):**
- Download Craftstrom app
- Create account
- Pair inverter with app

No special tools needed. You'll want a wrench, a voltage tester (for safety), and a ladder if mounting on a roof.

The trickiest part is choosing a good mounting location. You want south-facing (or as close as possible), good sun exposure, and safe access for mounting hardware.

Most people handle this fine. If you're not comfortable with anything above, call a local electrician to verify your outlet is GFCI-protected ($100–200).
